# VA Attorney Agent — Test Cases

Five realistic veteran narratives for testing the research pipeline.

## Case 1: PTSD Rating Increase (50% → 70%)

I did two tours in Afghanistan with the 82nd Airborne, 2009 and 2011. Got my PTSD rating at 50% back in 2018 and honestly thought I'd just live with it. But things have gotten worse, not better, and my VSO told me I should file for an increase.

Right now I'm having panic attacks almost every day. Not the once-a-week stuff they used to document — I mean I had three in one week last month. I had to pull over on the highway twice because I can't drive on it anymore. The on-ramps trigger me bad. My wife finally moved into the guest room because my nightmares wake her up and I've woken up screaming and grabbed her arm twice without knowing what I was doing. She's scared of me and that's the worst part.

At work I'm a site supervisor for a construction company. My guys know something is wrong. I've had two formal write-ups this year for blowing up at subcontractors over small stuff. My boss told me the next incident is termination. I missed 30 days of work this year between panic attacks, VA appointments, and days I just couldn't get out of the truck.

I'm on sertraline 200mg, prazosin for nightmares, and just started buspirone. I see a VA psychiatrist monthly and a private therapist biweekly. My therapist wrote a letter saying my condition has significantly worsened and that my occupational and social functioning is severely impaired. I submitted that with my increase claim. C&P exam was last Tuesday — examiner spent maybe 25 minutes with me, asked a checklist of questions, didn't ask anything about my work situation or my marriage. The denial is going to come back saying I'm still 50%, I can already feel it.

## Case 2: TBI Initial Claim

I was a 0311 with 2/7 Marines in Helmand Province, 2015. On March 14th our patrol vehicle got hit by an RPG. I wasn't medevaced — our corpsman checked me out, I said I was fine, we kept going. There's nothing in my service record about that day. Two guys in my squad will back me up that it happened.

After I got out in 2017 I started getting headaches that put me in bed for two days at a time. I can't be in bright light without sunglasses even indoors. Loud noises — like a bar or a sporting event — give me a migraine and I have to leave. My girlfriend says I forget things I just said five minutes ago. I got fired from a warehouse job because I couldn't keep track of inventory counts correctly.

I finally saw a neurologist last year who did a full workup and diagnosed me with post-concussive syndrome, says it's consistent with blast exposure. He wrote a nexus letter connecting it to the RPG incident based on my lay statement and two buddy statements I got from guys who were there.

I never filed a TBI claim because I thought you needed medical records from the actual day it happened. My VSO just told me that's not true, that lay statements can establish the in-service event. I'm filing now. I don't know what rating to expect or how the headaches versus the cognitive stuff gets evaluated separately.

## Case 3: MST-Related PTSD (Unrated)

I was in the Navy from 2008 to 2014, IT specialist, stationed at NAS Pensacola. In 2010 I was sexually assaulted by a senior enlisted in my command. I reported it through my chain of command. He got a non-judicial punishment and was moved to a different unit. I was told to move on. I didn't get any mental health follow-up. I didn't push it because I was terrified of what it would do to my career.

I got out in 2014 with an honorable discharge. By 2016 I was in crisis. I've been in private therapy since then and was formally diagnosed with PTSD and Major Depressive Disorder in 2017. My therapist has been seeing me for six years and has written a nexus letter stating both diagnoses are directly connected to the in-service assault.

I never filed a VA claim because I thought I needed the official investigation records. I recently learned about the MST alternative evidence rules — that markers like behavior changes, requests for transfer, and performance record changes can substitute for direct documentation of the assault. I have emails I sent to a friend the week it happened, my therapist's complete records, and the nexus letter. The assailant's NJP record should exist somewhere in my service records but I've never requested it.

I want to file but I'm scared about the C&P process for MST claims. Can I request a female examiner? What does the VA actually look for in these cases? My current combined rating is 0% — I've never filed for anything.

## Case 4: TDIU

I'm 58, did 20 years in the National Guard, deployed to Iraq in 2004 with the 256th Brigade. I've got three service-connected conditions: lumbar degenerative disc disease with right-sided radiculopathy at 30%, left knee degenerative joint disease at 20%, and tinnitus at 10%. Combined rating is 54%, which VA rounds to 60%. I've been working in warehouse logistics for 15 years.

In November 2022 I had a bad fall at work reaching for a pallet. Herniated another disc. I had surgery but I've never gotten back to where I was. I can't stand for more than 20 minutes without the leg pain and numbness getting bad. I can't sit for more than 30 minutes either. My job requires me to be on the floor constantly and I literally cannot do it anymore. I've been on short-term disability since the fall and it runs out in two months.

Two doctors have written letters saying I am unable to sustain substantially gainful employment due to my service-connected lumbar and radiculopathy conditions. My orthopedic surgeon and my VA primary care doctor both wrote letters.

My VSO mentioned TDIU but said I might not qualify because I need either 60% for one condition or 70% combined. I'm at 60% combined but no single condition is at 60%. Does that disqualify me? What's the application process and what do I need to submit? I'm terrified about losing income in two months.

## Case 5: CUE (Clear and Unmistakable Error)

I filed a PTSD claim in 2014 after getting out of the Army. Was denied in January 2015. The decision said the evidence did not show a current diagnosis with a nexus to service. I was out of time to appeal and figured that was it.

Last month I was cleaning out a storage unit and found my complete copy of everything I submitted with that original claim. I had submitted a buddy statement from my squad leader, who wrote a detailed account of the combat incidents I described in my stressor statement. I also submitted a five-page nexus letter from a private psychologist who diagnosed me with PTSD and directly connected it to my service.

I looked at the 2015 rating decision again. The evidence considered section lists my service records, my separation physical, and my stressor statement. The buddy statement and the psychologist's nexus letter are not listed anywhere. They were not mentioned. They were not discussed. They were not weighed. They just don't exist in the decision.

Both the buddy — my former squad leader — and the psychologist are still alive and available. The psychologist still has her records. The squad leader says he'll do whatever I need.

I know the normal appeal window is long gone. My VSO mentioned something called CUE — Clear and Unmistakable Error — and said it might apply here because the VA ignored submitted evidence. Can I actually win this? What do I need to prove? Is there a time limit on CUE claims?
